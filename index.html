Puerto Rico

by Logan Valenti

THE TAÍNO

The Taíno were a Native American tribe that lived in Puerto Rico, Cuba, and much of the Lesser Antilles (the tiny islands east of Puerto Rico). Their origin is disputed, but many believed that they came to the Carribbean through “island-hopping.” Many Spanish and English words came from them, such as “juracán” (hurricane) and “barbacoa” (barbeque).

THE SPANISH

The Spanish came to the new world a year after Columbus made his maiden voyage and subsequently discovered the island. He named it "San Juan Bautista" or Saint John the Baptist. Soon after discovery, he enslaved the natives of the island, killing almost 90% of them in the process. The Spanish proceeded to rule the island from 1493 to 1898, losing the island in the Spanish-American War.

THE AFRICANS

Because of the horrendous conditions that the Spaniards put the native Taínos through, most of them died. Since they still needed slave labor, they imported slaves, many from Bahía in Brazil and from the African countries of Guinea-Bissau and South Africa. This then helped them become the Puerto Rico of today, or "rich port."
